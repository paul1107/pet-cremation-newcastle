What to Do When Your Pet Dies

A gentle guide to the first steps — when you're not sure where to begin.

If Your Pet Passes at Home

  1. Take your time. There's no rush. Sit with them as long as you need.
  2. Keep them cool. If a collection will be several hours away, wrap them in a blanket and place them in a cool room — or use cool packs wrapped in towels placed alongside (not on) their body. For very small pets, a cool room is sufficient for short periods.
  3. Contact us. Complete our contact form and we'll arrange collection — typically the same day.
  4. Decide what you'd like. Individual or communal cremation? Memorial products? We'll guide you through the options — no pressure. See our answers to common pet cremation questions, our 2026 costs guide, or our guide to what actually happens during pet cremation.

If Your Pet Passes at the Vet

  1. Tell your vet you'd like us to handle the cremation. Most local practices know us and will call us directly.
  2. We collect from the practice — no need for you to transport your companion.
  3. We'll contact you to discuss cremation options, memorial products, and when you'd like the ashes returned.

Every situation is different. If you're unsure about anything — what type of cremation to choose, what happens during the process, how long things take — just get in touch. We'll talk you through it with patience and without any pressure.
