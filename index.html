What to Expect from a Transparent Referral

What this site does — and what the participating crematorium does.

Individual, Never Mixed

Ask the provider about its traceability procedure, identification system and certificate before you book. Those operational promises belong to the crematorium carrying out the service, not to this website.

No Pressure

This site is here to explain the choices without pushing a particular product or provider. Take the time you need and ask the crematorium any questions before agreeing.

Provider-arranged Collection

A participating crematorium may offer collection from home or a veterinary practice. The provider confirms its service area, availability and timing directly with you.

What to Do When Your Pet Dies

A gentle guide to the first steps — when you're not sure where to begin.

If Your Pet Passes at Home

  1. Take your time. There's no rush. Sit with them as long as you need.
  2. Keep them cool. If a collection will be several hours away, wrap them in a blanket and place them in a cool room — or use cool packs wrapped in towels placed alongside (not on) their body. For very small pets, a cool room is sufficient for short periods.
  3. Ask about availability. Complete our contact form. We will review the enquiry; no provider receives your details until you know who is available and agree to the referral.
  4. Decide what you'd like. Individual or communal cremation? Memorial products? We will guide you through the options — no pressure. See our answers to common pet cremation questions, our 2026 costs guide, our guide to what actually happens during pet cremation, or our explanation of individual pet cremation.

If Your Pet Passes at the Vet

  1. Tell your vet whether you already have a provider in mind. If you use this site, we will explain whether a participating provider is available.
  2. You decide whether you want your details passed to that provider. The provider then handles collection, cremation and ash return under its own terms.

Every situation is different. If you're unsure about anything — what type of cremation to choose, what happens during the process, how long things take — just get in touch. We will discuss you through it with patience and without any pressure.
