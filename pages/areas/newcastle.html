What to Do When Your Pet Dies at Home in Newcastle

A gentle guide for the first hours — when you're not sure what comes next.

1. Take Your Time

There is no rush. Sit with your companion as long as you need. This is your time to say goodbye, and nobody will hurry you. If your pet passes overnight, keep them in a cool, quiet room and contact us in the morning — we're available to collect 7 days a week.

2. Keep Them Cool

Wrap your companion in a soft blanket and place them in a cool, dry room — a tiled floor or utility room works well. For small pets, a cool room is sufficient for short periods. If collection will be several hours away, place ice packs wrapped in a towel alongside the body (never directly on). This prevents rapid changes while you make arrangements.

3. Contact Us

Complete our contact form and we'll call you back — typically within the hour during working hours. We'll arrange a collection time that suits you. Same-day collection is nearly always possible for all Newcastle postcodes (NE1–NE7, NE12, NE13, NE15).

4. Decide What You'd Like

Individual cremation means your companion is cremated alone and their ashes are returned to you in a hand-finished casket or scatter tube. Communal cremation is a dignified farewell where the combined ashes are scattered in a memorial garden. Not sure which to choose? We'll talk you through the options — no pressure. Newcastle Pet Cremation — Common Questions

Can I bury my pet in my garden in Newcastle?

Yes — in England it is legal to bury small domestic pets in your garden provided you own the property, the burial site is away from water sources and at least 1 metre deep, and your companion has not been treated with controlled drugs. Renting? You'll need written permission from your landlord. Many Newcastle families prefer cremation for the flexibility it offers — you can keep the ashes, scatter them in a favourite walking spot, or have them placed in the crematorium's memorial garden. If you're unsure which is right for you, we're happy to talk it through.

How long after a pet dies are they cremated?

In Newcastle, pets are typically cremated within 1 to 5 days after collection. For individual cremations, the ashes are usually returned within seven days — often sooner. The exact timing depends on the crematorium's schedule and the day of collection. We'll give you a clear timeline when we call and keep you updated throughout.

Do vets in Newcastle arrange pet cremation?

Yes — most Newcastle veterinary practices will offer to arrange cremation on your behalf, but you always have the right to choose your own provider. If you'd prefer us, simply tell your vet. We'll collect directly from the practice — no extra charge, and you don't need to be there. Many local vets in Jesmond, Gosforth, Heaton, and the city centre already know us and call us directly.
