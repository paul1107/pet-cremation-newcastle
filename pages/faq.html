Frequently Asked Questions

Honest answers to the questions families ask most often.

How do I know I'm getting my own companion's ashes back?

Ask the named crematorium to describe its traceability protocol before booking. Reputable local providers use a unique identification disc that travels with the body, follow single-pet-per-chamber protocols, and provide a certificate of individual cremation. These operational details belong to the crematorium — not to this referral site.

How quickly can you collect my companion?

Typically quick, often same-day — arranged with you. For Newcastle upon Tyne, Gateshead, North Tyneside, and South Tyneside, same-day collection is nearly always possible. For wider Northumberland, Sunderland, Durham, and further afield, collection is usually arranged with you. Participating providers' opening hours vary; ask at enquiry.

How long until I get the ashes back?

For individual cremation, ashes are typically returned arranged with you, typically within seven days. The provider calls you when they're ready to arrange hand-delivery at a time that suits you. If you need them sooner, let us know and we'll do our best to accommodate.

Do I have to pay upfront?

Ask the named provider for its payment terms. Reputable crematoriums typically invoice after the service.

Can you collect from my vet?

Yes. If your companion passes at the vet — or is already there — the participating crematorium may collect directly from the practice. Let your vet know you've chosen a provider and they will liaise. Vet practices often know which local providers families use.

Can I bring my companion to you myself?

Yes. Some families prefer to bring their companion themselves. Ask the participating crematorium whether it offers private appointment-only drop-off. Full location details come from the provider once a booking is confirmed.

Can I be there during the cremation?

Ask the named crematorium about attended or witnessed cremations and private time before the cremation. Practices vary.

What happens with communal cremation?

In communal cremation, your companion is cremated alongside a small number of other pets in a shared ceremony. Ask the named provider where and how the combined ashes are scattered. For a fuller comparison of communal and individual cremation, see our full guide.

Can I get a paw print even if I choose communal cremation?

Yes. Ask the participating crematorium whether it offers a paw-print impression before cremation.

What happens if my companion passes at night or on a weekend?

We're opening hours vary by provider; ask at enquiry. Outside office hours (9am–5pm weekdays, 10am–2pm Saturdays), leave a message and the provider calls you back — typically during opening hours. We can arrange collection even on Sundays and bank holidays. For a detailed walk-through of every stage, see what happens during pet cremation.

Do you handle large dogs?

Yes. We have the facilities to handle dogs of all sizes, including extra-large breeds (45 kg+). Pricing is based on weight bands — see our pricing page for details.

What areas do you cover?

We serve Newcastle upon Tyne, Gateshead, North Tyneside, South Tyneside, Sunderland, Northumberland, County Durham, Hexham, Morpeth, and the wider North East. Collection fees vary by distance — see our pricing page.
