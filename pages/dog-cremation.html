Dog Cremation in Newcastle upon Tyne

A gentle, dignified farewell for your dog — every breed, every size. Individual cremation, prompt collection, arranged with you.

Every Breed, Every Size

Dogs come in all shapes and sizes — and so does saying goodbye. We provide dog cremation in Newcastle for every breed, from the smallest toy terrier to the largest Great Dane. Our process is the same careful, traceable service for all dogs, but we understand the loss is different for every family.

For many people, losing a dog means losing a daily presence — the morning walk, the greeting at the door, the quiet companion on the sofa. We help you understand the practical side so you can decide what matters.

Our size bands:

  • Toy & small breeds (under 10 kg) — Jack Russell, Yorkshire Terrier, small terriers, miniature breeds
  • Medium breeds (10–25 kg) — Border Collie, Springer Spaniel, Cocker Spaniel, Beagle
  • Large breeds (25–45 kg) — Labrador, Retriever, German Shepherd, Boxer
  • Extra-large breeds (45 kg+) — Great Dane, Mastiff, Bernese Mountain Dog, Irish Wolfhound

Common Questions from Dog Owners

How quickly can you collect?

Collection is typically same-day across Newcastle and the surrounding areas. We arrange the time with you — there is no rush on your side, and the crematorium arrives when it feels right.

Can my other dog say goodbye?

Yes. Many families find it helps their remaining dog to see and sniff their companion. The participating crematorium typically gives you whatever time you need before collection.

What about very large breeds?

The crematorium handles dogs of all sizes — including Great Danes, Mastiffs, and other extra-large breeds. Larger dogs need a bigger chamber, which is reflected in the price. We'll quote honestly upfront.

Is individual cremation guaranteed?

Yes. Your dog is cremated alone in a dedicated chamber. A unique ID disc follows them through every stage, and you receive a certificate confirming individual cremation.

Can I keep some ashes and scatter the rest?

Yes. Many families divide the ashes — some kept at home in a small urn, some scattered in a favourite walk. We can split the ashes for you, or you can do this yourself.

What if my dog has died at the vet?

We can collect directly from your vet's practice. Many local vets know us well and will call us on your behalf — just let them know you'd like us to handle the cremation.
