What to Expect

Step by step — from the moment you call us to the day the ashes come home.

Step 1 — You Call Us

When your companion passes — or if you know it's coming soon — call us on 0191 000 0000 or fill in our contact form. We'll answer gently, listen, and guide you through the options. There's no rush and no pressure. We'll ask a few simple questions:

  • Your companion's name and species
  • Approximate weight
  • Where they are now (home, vet, or other)
  • Whether you'd like individual or communal cremation
  • Any memorial products you're interested in

Step 2 — We Collect

We'll agree a collection time that works for you — typically within 2 hours in Newcastle, same-day in most cases. We arrive in an unmarked vehicle. Our team member will introduce themselves quietly, handle your companion with the utmost care, and give you whatever time you need to say goodbye.

If collecting from your vet, we liaise directly with the practice — you don't need to be there. Simply tell your vet you've chosen us and we'll handle the rest.

Step 3 — Cremation

For individual cremation, your companion is cremated alone in a clean chamber. A unique identification disc stays with them at every stage — from collection to ash return — so you can be certain the ashes you receive are theirs.

For communal cremation, your companion is cremated alongside a small number of others in a shared, dignified ceremony. The combined ashes are scattered with care in our memorial garden.

Step 4 — Your Companion Comes Home

For individual cremation, we'll contact you when the ashes are ready — usually within 2–3 days. We hand-deliver them to your door in your chosen casket or scatter tube, along with the certificate of individual cremation.

If you opted for memorial products like a paw-print keepsake or engraved plaque, these arrive with the ashes.

For communal cremation, we'll send your certificate and, if you'd like, let you know when the ashes have been scattered in the garden so you can visit.
